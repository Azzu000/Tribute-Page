Jane Goodall

World's Foremost Expert on Chimpanzees

Jane Goodall (Image reference: Jane Goodall Institute)

Throughout most of Jane Goodall's life she have dedicated herself to studying chimpanzees. While she cannot interfere with their lives, she lived near them and embraced their pressence. After the research, she continued dedicated her life in fighting for chimpanzee conservation through the Jane Goodall Institute.
  • 1934 Born in London, England.
  • 1959 Invited to join the research project in Tanzania by anthropologist Louis Leakey to study chimpanzees as little was known about the little creature back then.
  • 1960 Traveled from Long, England to Tanzania to study chimpanzees at the Gombe Stream National Park.
  • 1962 Accepted into Cambridge University in the UK for a phD in ethology.
  • 1963 Finished and publish her first book "My Life Among Wild Chimpanzees."
  • 1965 Completed the thesis for her phD in ethology
  • 1977 Founded the Jane Goodall Institution to fight against human environmental destruction
  • 1991 Founded Roots and Shoots program to educate the younger generation about environmental presesrvation
  • 2002 Appointed to be the United Nations Messenger of Peace
  • 2025 She is now 91 years old and continues her work in environment preservation and chimpanzee preservation
